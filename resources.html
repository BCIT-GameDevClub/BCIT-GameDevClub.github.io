+ Assets

It can be immensely helpful to grab assets that are already made, whether it's for a quick proof-of-concept or a finished game. Unless otherwise noted, the sites listed provide free assets, though some impose usage restrictions.

  • OpenGameArt - Free and open-source assets of all types.
  • Wikimedia Commons - A parallel project to Wikipedia with various media.
  • itch.io - The assets section of a great indie game site. (free and paid)
  • Game Dev Market - An asset marketplace tailored toward indies. (paid)
  • Unity Asset Store - The official asset marketplace for the Unity engine. (free and paid)

+ Engines

Simple Engines

These generally require no programming and offer a drag-and-drop interface. These engines are very accessible but are limited and some target a specific genre of game.

  • Game Maker
  • Construct 2
  • Stencyl
  • Twine
  • RPG Maker
  • Craft Studio

Full Engines

More powerful engines that have been used successfully for indie games. Some are more complex and less accessible than others.

  • Unity 3D
  • Godot
  • Duality
  • Unreal Engine 4
  • Torque 3d

Esoteric Engines

Unusual engines that may make things harder instead of easier but offer unique style or appeal.

  • GZDoom
  • Pico 8
  • Voxatron

Frameworks and Libraries

These are not engines that can be used out of the box, but toolkits that can make writing a game much easier.

  • libgdx (Java)
  • Love2D (Lua)
  • Phaser (JS)
  • Babylon.js (JS)
  • Polycode (JS)
  • Pygame (Python)
